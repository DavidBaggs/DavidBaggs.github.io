Misrepresentation of Native Americans in Thanksgiving Narratives

Introduction

People’s lack of knowledge about Native Americans is one of the biggest problems when it comes to misrepresentation. America’s oblivion to Native American culture leads to insensitive behaviors such as stereotyping and appropriation. One of the main forms of this inaccurate categorization of Native Americans is present during Thanksgiving time.

Do Americans misrepresent Native American culture during Thanksgiving?

“It’s always portrayed as Natives saving some colony from starvation and everyone getting along. But if you look into the facts of that whole interaction, it wasn’t that good.

- Professor James Florendo

Stereotyping in School Plays

Phrases like “The Indians” in elementary school Thanksgiving plays overgeneralizes the entire population of Native Americans

"Native Americans" all dressed in costumes that were made to look old and dirty, along with inauthentic, appropriated headdresses.

Native Americans would run around a fake campfire like a group of savages feeding right into the stereotypes of the primal Native American that is often shown in the media
